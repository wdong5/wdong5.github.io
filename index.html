Assistant Professor

Wenqian Dong

School of EECS, Oregon State University (Fall 2024–)

Google Scholar Lab website LinkedIn OSU profile

Her research interests include high-performance computing (HPC), machine learning systems (MLSys), and AI for Science (AI4S). Her work has been published in top-tier venues such as SC, HPDC, ASPLOS, ICS, EuroSys, and VLDB. She earned her Ph.D. from the University of California, Merced, in 2022, under the supervision of Prof. Dong Li, and is the recipient of the IEEE Computer Society TCHPC Early Career Researchers Award for Excellence in High-Performance Computing (2023). She leads the Parallel Intelligent Computing (PiComp) Lab and co-leads the High-Performance Computing and Systems at Oregon State (HipCastor) Lab.

Prior to OSU, she was a research intern in the High-Performance Computing Group at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL) and in the AI Labs at Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE); her work at PNNL was highlighted by DOE Newswise and the PNNL website. She is also a recipient of the Bobcat Fellowship at UC Merced (2020, 2018).

Recruiting: If you are interested in applying to the Ph.D. program (open for graduates/senior undergrads) in my group or remote research intern (open for undergrads) from my group, please send your CV and transcript to my OSU email box wenqian.dong[at]oregonstate.edu.
